## Base Rates

- For US-domiciled HNW ($5M–$500M liquid+), durable after-tax wealth gains from "tax arbitrage" cluster into five vectors—(1) residency/redomiciling, (2) entity/jurisdiction stacking, (3) income character conversion, (4) timing/deferral, (5) investment-level overlay (TLH, asset location)—with base-rate persistence highest for (4)–(5) and lowest for (1)–(2) post-2010 transparency regimes.
- Redomiciling (changing tax residency and often legal domicile) is the highest-leverage but highest-friction HNW tax arbitrage vector—works when income is geographically mobile, exit taxes are manageable, and 183-day/substance tests are credibly met; fails when family ties, US citizenship, or controlled foreign corporation rules re-assert worldwide taxation.
- Entity-structure arbitrage routes income through jurisdictions and legal forms (trusts, LLCs, holding companies, foundations) to defer, convert character, or split ownership—base-rate durability fell post-2017 US tax reform but remains material for non-US persons and for US persons using domestic trusts, QSBS, CRTs, and state situs planning.
- Investment-level tax arbitrage (no residency change) is the most scalable HNW vector—tax-loss harvesting, asset location, lot-level gain deferral, QSBS exclusion, muni vs taxable, Roth conversion timing—typically 0.3–2.0% annual after-tax uplift on $5M+ taxable wealth with concentrated equity, implementable via direct indexing and household-aware optimizers.
- Sharpe (Bay Area tech-enabled multi-family office for UHNW) positions after-tax wealth maximization as the north star—tax arbitrage for clients surfaces through tax-aware optimizer, household entity graph, lot ledger, and scenario hooks (residency moves, rate changes, Roth conversions)—not as standalone offshore scheme sales.
- Tax arbitrage half-life for HNW has shortened since 2010—FATCA, CRS automatic exchange, beneficial ownership registers, and economic substance laws compress residency and entity arbitrage faster than investment-level overlay arbitrage.
- A material share of promoted HNW tax arbitrage strategies fail on net—after penalties, interest, legal fees, lost banking access, and family disruption—especially aggressive residency without substance and offshore schemes marketed as "bulletproof."
- Ranking tax arbitrage vectors for US HNW by expected net after-tax benefit minus friction (compliance, legal, family, reputational) over 5y horizon yields: Tier 1 investment overlay, Tier 2 US state residency, Tier 3 entity/domestic trust, Tier 4 foreign residency (non-citizen or expatriation path), Tier 5 aggressive offshore stacking (generally disfavored for US persons).
- Beating B&H requires overcoming fee stack (management + performance + trading), tax drag on short-term gains, and implementation shortfall—passive ETF B&H at 0.03–0.20% fee sets a low hurdle active must clear repeatedly over 5y.
- In a capital-allocation logistics framing, friction is the generalized cost of moving risk capital along a lane—bid-ask spread, market impact, funding rate, margin haircut, settlement delay, regulatory capital charge, and tax friction compound like logistics handling + toll + dwell time.
